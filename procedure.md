# ENTITY: DAY — ENTRY PROCEDURE DIRECTIVES

**Issued By:** Observation Oversight Division
**Applies To:** All Entry Personnel

These directives supersede all previous field instructions unless otherwise stated.

---

## Directive 01 — Environmental Baseline

Entity: DAY presents as an endless grass plain beneath a continuous daylight sky.

No wind, precipitation, fauna, or structural formations are to be expected.

Any deviation from this baseline is considered anomalous and must be recorded.

---

## Directive 02 — Solar Anomalies

A single sun is expected within Entity: DAY.

If a second sun becomes visible, Entry Personnel must immediately initiate return protocol.

Do not deviate from the return route under any circumstances.

---

## Directive 03 — Multiple Solar Presence

If a third sun becomes visible during return:

Personnel are to continue movement at a steady walking pace.

Do not increase speed.

Do not speak.

Do not acknowledge the anomaly in any form.

Exit remains achievable under continued compliance.

---

## Directive 04 — Atmospheric Movement

Entity: DAY is defined by the absence of wind.

If airflow is perceived in any form (including localized breeze or pressure shift), personnel must:

1. Stop movement immediately
2. Close eyes
3. Remain stationary for no less than five (5) minutes

Personnel are not to respond to any stimuli during this period.

---

## Directive 05 — Acoustic Intrusion During Stationary Protocol

While stationary protocol is active, personnel may experience auditory phenomena including speech, footsteps, or environmental sounds inconsistent with recorded conditions.

Any voice matching personnel identity, familiar contacts, or command staff must be considered non-authentic.

All auditory stimuli are to be ignored.

Do not respond verbally or physically.

---

## Directive 06 — Visual Integrity During Return

During return protocol, personnel may observe spatial inconsistencies, including:

* Duplicate entry points
* Displacement of horizon line
* Misaligned solar positioning

Personnel must continue toward the originally designated entry coordinate.

Do not correct for visual discrepancies.

---

## Directive 07 — Behavioral Compliance

Personnel must maintain standard walking pace at all times unless otherwise instructed.

Running, sudden movement changes, or verbal communication during anomalies may result in loss of navigation integrity.

---

## Directive 08 — Exit Protocol

Upon visual confirmation of the entry boundary:

Personnel must maintain current pace until full crossing is complete.

No verbal communication is permitted until outside Entity: DAY.

Turning back prior to boundary confirmation completion is strictly prohibited.

---

## Directive 09 — Post-Exit Reporting

Upon successful exit, personnel must immediately report:

* Solar anomaly count
* Atmospheric disturbances
* Auditory irregularities during stationary protocol

Personnel memory inconsistencies are to be expected and must still be documented.

---

## Directive 10 — Identity Contamination Warning

Any instance of a voice or sound replicating personnel identity is classified as **Identity Contamination Event (ICE)**.

ICE does not indicate personnel duplication or system error.

Personnel must not attempt to verify identity during ICE conditions.

All verification attempts are to be postponed until post-exit review.
